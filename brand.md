## 1. Brand Foundation

### Who We Are

CreatrOps is a creator operations service — a dedicated ops team that handles the business side of brand partnerships so creators can focus on creating. We sit at the intersection of operational excellence and creator culture. We are not a tool. We are not an agency. We are not a management company. We are a specialist operations function that runs quietly and effectively in the background.

### Our Positioning

**For creators and influencers** who have real brand partnership activity but no formal operations infrastructure, CreatrOps is the dedicated ops team that handles every inquiry, qualifies every opportunity, manages every deal, and ensures nothing falls through the cracks — so the creator never has to think about the business side again.

### Tagline

**"Your brand deals. Finally running themselves."**

### Mission

To give every creator — regardless of size — access to the operational infrastructure that was previously only available to those with major management deals or agency representation.

### Brand Personality

CreatrOps has a clear, consistent personality. Every piece of communication, every design decision, every word choice should reflect these traits:

- **Authoritative** — We know what we're doing. We don't hedge, qualify unnecessarily, or oversell. We state things plainly and confidently.
- **Exclusive** — We are not for everyone. We are for creators who are serious about their business. Access feels earned, not given.
- **Invisible** — The best ops team is one you never have to think about. We work in the background. We don't need credit.
- **Precise** — We are detail-oriented. Sloppy communication, vague promises, or imprecise language is off-brand.
- **Warm but not casual** — We are professional without being cold. We treat creators like the business owners they are.

### What CreatrOps Is Not

- Not a creative agency — we do not touch content, creative direction, or publishing
- Not a talent management company — we do not negotiate on behalf of creators in a legally regulated capacity
- Not a tech platform — we are a service that happens to use technology
- Not a marketing service — we do not grow audiences or manage social presence
- Not a legal or financial advisor

## 2. Visual Identity

### Color Palette

The CreatrOps palette is built on deep black, crimson red, and warm gold. These three anchors define the luxury and exclusivity of the brand. White is used sparingly as a text and accent color only — never as a background.

#### Primary Colors

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Obsidian** | `#050505` | Primary background |
| **Obsidian 2** | `#0D0D0D` | Secondary background, section alternates |
| **Obsidian 3** | `#141414` | Cards, elevated surfaces |
| **Obsidian 4** | `#1A1A1A` | Input fields, deep surface |
| **Crimson** | `#C8102E` | Primary accent, CTAs, highlights |
| **Crimson Deep** | `#8B0000` | Hover states, shadows |
| **Crimson Bright** | `#E8001A` | Active states, emphasis |

#### Secondary Colors

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Gold** | `#C9A84C` | Premium accents, badges, dividers |
| **Gold Light** | `#E8C97A` | Italic emphasis, featured text |
| **White** | `#FAFAFA` | Body text, headings on dark |
| **White Muted** | `#B0A89A` | Secondary text, labels, captions |

#### Color Rules

- **Never** use white as a background color
- **Never** use bright colors on bright backgrounds
- **Never** use more than three colors in a single composition
- Gold and Crimson should not compete — use one as dominant, one as accent
- All backgrounds must be dark. CreatrOps does not have a light mode.

### Typography

CreatrOps uses a three-font system. Each font has a specific role and must not be substituted.

#### Display — Cormorant Garamond

Used for: headlines, hero text, section titles, pricing figures, editorial moments

- Style: serif, elegant, editorial
- Weights used: 300 (Light), 400 (Regular), 600 (Semi-bold)
- Italic is used intentionally for emphasis and brand moments
- Never use Cormorant Garamond for body copy or UI labels
- Minimum size: 22px

```
font-family: 'Cormorant Garamond', serif;
```

#### Impact — Bebas Neue

Used for: statistics, large numbers, logo, eyebrow labels, dramatic callouts

- Style: condensed sans-serif, bold, cinematic
- Weight: 400 (only weight available)
- Always uppercase
- Never use for body copy or long-form text
- Minimum size: 18px

```
font-family: 'Bebas Neue', sans-serif;
```

#### Body — Montserrat

Used for: body copy, UI labels, navigation, buttons, form fields, captions

- Style: geometric sans-serif, clean, modern
- Weights used: 300 (Light), 400 (Regular), 500 (Medium), 600 (Semi-bold)
- Letter-spacing: 0.3px for body, 2-5px for uppercase labels
- Default weight is 300 — CreatrOps copy is light, not heavy

```
font-family: 'Montserrat', sans-serif;
```

#### Type Scale

| Element | Font | Size | Weight | Letter Spacing |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hero headline | Cormorant Garamond | 52–96px (clamp) | 300 | -1px |
| Section title | Cormorant Garamond | 36–60px (clamp) | 300 | 0 |
| Card title | Cormorant Garamond | 22px | 400 | 0 |
| Stat number | Bebas Neue | 48–80px | 400 | 2px |
| Price | Cormorant Garamond | 48–56px | 300 | 0 |
| Logo | Bebas Neue | 22–36px | 400 | 4–7px |
| Section label | Montserrat | 10px | 400 | 5px |
| Body copy | Montserrat | 13–14px | 300 | 0.3px |
| UI label | Montserrat | 9–11px | 400 | 3px |
| Button | Montserrat | 10–11px | 500 | 3–4px |
| Caption | Montserrat | 9–10px | 300 | 2px |

#### Typography Rules

- Headlines are never all-caps (that's Bebas Neue's job)
- Italic in Cormorant Garamond is intentional and used for brand moments, not general emphasis
- Button text is always uppercase, always Montserrat, always tracked out
- Body copy line height: 1.8–1.9
- Never bold body copy — use weight 500 Montserrat sparingly for emphasis

### Logo

The CreatrOps wordmark is set in Bebas Neue with wide letter-spacing. "Creatr" is white, "Ops" is Crimson (`#C8102E`).

```
CreatrOps
```

- Always "CreatrOps" — never "Creatr Ops", "CREATROPS", or "creatrops"
- The color split between "Creatr" and "Ops" must always be maintained
- Minimum size: 20px
- Clear space: equal to the height of the "C" on all sides
- Never place on a light background
- Never stretch, rotate, or recolor

#### Subline

When the parent brand attribution is needed:

```
CreatrOps
by Clairen Haus
```

The subline is set in Montserrat, 8–10px, letter-spacing 5px, uppercase, in Gold (`#C9A84C`).

### Spacing & Layout

- Generous negative space is a brand signal — crowded layouts are off-brand
- Section padding: 80–120px vertical on desktop, 60px on mobile
- Max content width: 1200px (wide sections), 1100px (standard), 720px (narrow/form)
- Grid gaps: 24px (cards), 80px (two-column), 1px (dividers using background color trick)

### Motion & Animation

Motion is used deliberately — not decoratively.

- **Entrance animations:** `fadeUp` — opacity 0 to 1, translateY 30px to 0, duration 0.9s, cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)
- **Hover states:** subtle transform (translateY -4px), border color shifts, opacity changes — never scale or rotate
- **Ambient effects:** radial gradients that pulse slowly, scan lines, shimmer sweeps — all slow and cinematic
- **No** bounce, elastic, or playful easing — everything is smooth and controlled
- **No** rapid or distracting animations — if it calls attention to itself it's wrong

### Texture & Atmosphere

CreatrOps pages have depth and atmosphere, not flat color. Standard techniques:

- **Noise grain overlay:** subtle SVG fractalNoise at low opacity (0.035) fixed over the page
- **Radial glows:** deep red or gold radial gradients bleeding in from edges or bottoms
- **Grid lines:** very faint red grid at low opacity, masked to fade at edges
- **Satin effect on cards:** diagonal gradient sheen that sweeps across slowly
- **Corner accents:** thin 1px L-shaped corner marks in gold or red on cards

## 3. Voice & Tone

### How CreatrOps Speaks

CreatrOps speaks like a high-end specialist who has seen everything and doesn't need to impress anyone. Confident without being arrogant. Direct without being cold. Premium without being pretentious.

Think: a boutique law firm, a private wealth manager, a top-tier talent agency. Not a startup. Not a SaaS product. Not a hustle-culture brand.

### Voice Pillars

**Direct.** We say what we mean. No filler, no hedging, no corporate speak.
> ✓ "Your ops team handles every inquiry."
> ✗ "Our platform helps facilitate the management of inbound opportunities."

**Confident.** We don't qualify unnecessarily. We know this works.
> ✓ "Nothing falls through the cracks."
> ✗ "We try our best to make sure most things don't get missed."

**Elevated.** We write like a luxury brand, not a tech startup.
> ✓ "Your brand deals. Finally running themselves."
> ✗ "Automate your influencer brand deal workflow!"

**Human.** Despite the luxury positioning, we are not robotic or stiff.
> ✓ "You built the audience. The business is bleeding."
> ✗ "Creators experience suboptimal operational efficiency."

### Tone by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Landing page headlines | Dramatic, punchy, confident |
| Landing page body | Clear, empathetic, authoritative |
| Email communications (as persona) | Professional, warm, concise |
| Error messages | Calm, helpful, never alarming |
| Onboarding wizard | Encouraging, clear, frictionless |
| Monthly reports | Factual, clear, business-like |
| Escalation notifications | Neutral, informative, action-oriented |

### Words We Use

- Ops team / operations team
- Account manager
- Deal pipeline
- Brand partnership
- Inquiry / opportunity
- Handled / covered / managed
- Activate / live / operational
- Dedicated / specialist
- Professional / precise

### Words We Never Use

- AI / artificial intelligence / machine learning / agents (in client-facing copy)
- Automated / bot / robot
- Platform / tool / software (we are a service)
- Hustle / grind / crush it
- Revolutionary / game-changing / disruptive
- Synergy / leverage (as a verb) / scalable solutions
- Amazing / awesome / incredible
- We're here to help / just reach out / don't hesitate

### Punctuation & Style

- Em dashes (—) used sparingly for dramatic pauses in headlines
- Ellipses never used — they imply uncertainty
- Exclamation points never used in brand copy
- Sentence case for all headlines (not Title Case)
- Numbers under 10 are spelled out in body copy, numerals in stats and UI
- Dollar amounts always include comma formatting: $1,250 not $1250
- Percentages: 20% not 20 percent in UI, spelled out in formal documents

## 4. Brand Applications

### Landing Page

- Dark background always
- Hero headline in Cormorant Garamond, large, light weight
- Stats in Bebas Neue, Crimson
- Section labels in Montserrat, 10px, letter-spaced, Crimson
- CTAs always in Crimson, Montserrat, uppercase, tracked
- Cards have subtle top line gradient (Crimson or Gold)
- Glows bleed in from bottom of cards and sections

### Email (Persona Communications)

- Sent as the assigned persona (e.g. Sarah Chen) — never as CreatrOps or Clairen Haus
- Plain text preferred for deliverability — minimal HTML
- Signature: persona name, title, email address only — no logo in email signature
- Tone: professional, warm, concise — under 150 words unless situation requires more
- No em dashes in email copy — they can render as `—` in some clients
- No "I hope this email finds you well" or similar filler openers
- No references to CreatrOps, Clairen Haus, or any tooling

### Creator Portal

- Same dark luxury aesthetic as landing page
- Navigation: minimal, left sidebar or top bar
- Data presented cleanly — numbers are the hero
- Section labels and status indicators in Montserrat uppercase
- Deal stages shown as a clean pipeline, not a table
- Monthly reports use the brand typography and palette

### Contracts & Documents

- Clean, professional formatting — not styled like the marketing site
- CreatrOps name appears only in the header/footer as "CreatrOps by Clairen Haus"
- Clairen Haus LLC as the legal entity name
- Standard business document conventions — not branded aggressively

### Social Media (CreatrOps Brand Accounts)

- Same voice and tone as landing page
- Dark backgrounds or black cards with brand typography
- No stock photography — abstract, atmospheric visuals only
- Stats and social proof lead — features follow
- Never reveal the operational/AI infrastructure in public posts

## 5. Affiliate & Partner Brand Guidelines

### For Standard Affiliates

When promoting CreatrOps, affiliates must:

- Accurately represent the service as described in official materials
- Disclose their affiliate relationship clearly and conspicuously per FTC guidelines
- Use only approved language and descriptions
- Never claim specific revenue results or guarantees on behalf of CreatrOps

Affiliates must not:

- Mention AI, automation, or agent technology in their promotions
- Make claims beyond what is in official CreatrOps materials
- Use the CreatrOps or Clairen Haus logo without written permission
- Disparage competitors or make comparative claims

### Approved Affiliate Language

> "CreatrOps gave me a dedicated ops team for my brand partnerships — they handle everything from inbound to contracts. Use my link to get early access."

> "I stopped losing brand deals when I got CreatrOps. My account manager handles every inquiry so I can focus on creating."

### Not Approved

> "CreatrOps uses AI to automate your DMs!" ✗
> "Make $10,000 a month guaranteed with CreatrOps!" ✗
> "This bot handles all your brand deals!" ✗
